## AI Slop Verdict
Not slop — this reads as genuinely hand-built by someone who understands the constraints. The token system, the honest-metadata discipline (no fabricated progress bars when duration is unknown, CWCard.web line 207), the calm benefit-first fatal-error copy (VideoPlayerScreen.web line 341), and the deferred/undoable removal (HistoryScreen.web line 339) are all things a Linear/Stripe-fluent user would recognize as care. Where they WOULD pause: (1) keyboard focus on desktop is invisible or unreachable in several places — tabbing produces nothing or a raw UA outline, which reads as "unfinished" instantly; (2) native browser confirm()/alert() dialogs (AccountsScreen line 23-41) puncture the dark-theater aesthetic with a white OS box at exactly the wrong moment; (3) three different poster-card components with subtly different hover/skeleton behavior across Movies/Series/Home. These are "strangeness with a cause" more than generic slop, but they're the specific spots where trust wobbles.

## Cognitive Load
Intrinsic load is well-managed: browse rails, discover pills, and detail heroes keep one clear task per surface, and the featured hero gives a single obvious entry point. Extraneous load is mostly low but spikes in two places. First, the VideoPlayer 'Settings/tune' popover (VideoPlayerScreen.web:883) stacks speed, audio, quality, aspect, display toggles, subtitle tuning, picture adjust, and sleep timer into one scroll — grouped and labelled, so acceptable, but it's the one >4-options decision point and a first-timer will scan hard. Second, the persistent shortcut legend footer duplicates Settings and adds standing noise. Missing-state gaps add germane friction: full-screen/grid loads show spinners where the rails set a skeleton expectation, and the episode list has no hover/focus affordance so the 'which row is actionable' question is unanswered on desktop. No decision point exceeds a sensible option count except the intentionally-consolidated player menu.

## Emotional Journey
Peaks are real and well-placed: the Home hero as a cinematic 'pick up where you left off' moment, the calm 'Connected to X' banner that auto-returns (AccountsScreen:145), the Undo snackbar that quietly protects a mis-tap across synced devices, and the fatal-error screen that reassures before it diagnoses. The end-state (peak-end) of a successful play session is strong. The valleys cluster at setup and failure: a first-time user who mistypes credentials gets a white OS alert() box (AccountsScreen:124) — the single most aesthetically-jarring moment in the app, at the highest-stakes step. A keyboard-only user hits a wall immediately — the top nav can't be reached — which reads as broken, not minimal. A live-TV viewer reaching for the mouse to lower volume finds no control at all. Reassurance during flaky-network buffering is good (frozen-frame + Reconnecting…), except reduced-motion users see a stalled spinner that looks like a hang.

## Strengths
- Deferred, undoable removal with a live-region snackbar (HistoryScreen.web:339, useDeferredRemove) — treats a synced mis-tap as the real risk it is, and the 'Removed · Undo' pill is calm and reversible. This is product-grade thinking, not decoration.
- Honest-metadata discipline is applied everywhere: no fabricated progress bar when duration is unknown (CWCard.web:207, LiveCard EPG:154), quality/aspect chips reflect real engine state, and the fatal-error headline stays benefit-first while relegating the raw engine reason to quiet muted secondary text (VideoPlayerScreen.web:817).
- Deliberate 44×44 hit areas engineered around small glyphs (RemoveButton in HistoryScreen.web:100, the star toggle in LiveCard:127) plus skeleton posters that reserve the exact real footprint to eliminate layout shift (SkeletonPoster.web) — the small-target and CLS bars are met on purpose, not by accident.

## Priority Issues

### [P0] Top navigation is not reachable by keyboard on desktop web
- **File:** src/navigation/AppNavigator.web.jsx:204
- **Why:** NavLink renders a YStack with onPress but no tabIndex/role (AppNavigator.web:204-235), and the Accounts/Settings/Profile controls set role=button but no tabIndex (AppNavigator.web:335-418). None are keyboard-focusable, so a keyboard-only or AT user on web/Electron cannot switch tabs, open Accounts, open Settings, or switch profile at all. Fails WCAG 2.1.1 Keyboard — a hard a11y bar. The custom index-ring nav is TV-remote-only and does not help DOM keyboard users.
- **Fix:** Render nav items and the icon actions as real <button>s (or add tabIndex=0 + onKeyDown for Enter/Space) and draw the existing cyan accent2 focus ring on :focus-visible so keyboard focus is both reachable and visible.
- **Command:** $impeccable harden

### [P1] Keyboard focus is invisible/inconsistent on posters, pills, live and history cards
- **File:** src/presentation/components/PosterCard.web.jsx:51
- **Why:** PosterCard, DiscoverPills, LiveCard, CWCard, MyListCard, RemoveButton, and the shelf-title div are role=button tabIndex=0 divs whose cyan ring only shows when isFocused (TV D-pad) or on :hover — and hover is explicitly disabled during keyboard nav via body.keyboard-nav (AppNavigator.web:87). There is no :focus-visible rule for these classes, so a desktop Tab user gets either nothing or a raw UA outline, while Button/IconButton correctly draw the branded ring on focus. Focus treatment is split across the interactive vocabulary (WCAG 2.4.7).
- **Fix:** Add a shared :focus-visible rule that applies the same cyan ring+glow used for :hover to .suvo-poster-card / .suvo-discover-pill / .suvo-live-card / .suvo-cw-card (and mirror the Button/IconButton onFocus ring on the div-based controls), independent of the keyboard-nav hover suppression.
- **Command:** $impeccable harden

### [P1] Episode list rows are mouse-only: no hover, no keyboard access
- **File:** src/components/SeriesDetail.web.jsx:246
- **Why:** In SeriesDetail's episodes view the SectionList renderItem rows are YStacks with onPress and hoverStyle={{borderColor: accent}} (SeriesDetail.web:246-257), but the web primitives DROP hoverStyle (primitives.web:18) so there is no hover feedback, and the rows have no role/tabIndex so they can't be focused or fired by keyboard. Picking an episode is the core Series task, and on desktop it has no visible affordance and no keyboard path. The hoverStyle also uses indigo (accent), which violates the cyan-is-hover Single-Light rule even if it applied.
- **Fix:** Make each episode row a focusable button (role=button, tabIndex=0, Enter/Space handler) with a real :hover/:focus-visible border in cyan via a className rule, matching the poster/live-card interaction language.
- **Command:** $impeccable harden

### [P1] Live playback exposes no pointer volume, mute, or play/pause control
- **File:** src/screens/VideoPlayerScreen.web.jsx:1032
- **Why:** The entire seek/transport row — play/pause, mute, and volume slider — is gated behind tvDuration > 0 (VideoPlayerScreen.web:1032). Live streams report duration 0, so for live content none of these render; only subtitles/settings/fullscreen remain. Worse, keyboard ArrowUp/Down is remapped to channel up/down for live (line 585-593), so there is no volume or mute affordance at all for a live viewer using a mouse. A viewer in a quiet room cannot mute the channel from the UI.
- **Fix:** Render a play/pause + mute + volume cluster for live independent of tvDuration (omit only the seek track/time when duration is unknown), and keep a pointer mute control available on every stream type.
- **Command:** $impeccable adapt

### [P1] No-account empty state misdirects first-time setup to a nonexistent path
- **File:** src/screens/MoviesScreen.web.jsx:139
- **Why:** Movies and Series no-account panels read 'Add your media service from Settings' (MoviesScreen.web:139, SeriesScreen.web:389), but there is no add-service path in Settings — accounts are added in the Accounts dialog. A first-time user follows the instruction, opens Settings, finds only Playback/Shortcuts/About, and is stuck. The CTA button correctly navigates to Accounts, so the copy and the action disagree.
- **Fix:** Change the message to point at Accounts (e.g. 'Connect an account to start watching') and reuse the shared labels.js noAccount copy so all three surfaces match.
- **Command:** $impeccable clarify

### [P1] Native browser confirm()/alert() dialogs rupture the dark aesthetic at high-stakes moments
- **File:** src/screens/AccountsScreen.jsx:23
- **Why:** AccountsScreen falls back to globalThis.confirm/alert on web for delete, sign-out, and — critically — the 'Couldn't connect' credential error (AccountsScreen.web:23-41, 124). A white OS dialog box appears over the midnight theater UI at the single highest-stakes step of first-time setup (verifying a playlist), which reads as broken and off-brand, and reinvents a standard affordance the app already has (the styled StatePanel/inline banner pattern used for Add-Channel).
- **Fix:** Replace confirm()/alert() with the app's own inline banner / styled dialog vocabulary (the surface2 dialog + danger banner already used elsewhere) so destructive confirms and connect errors stay in-theme.
- **Command:** $impeccable harden

### [P1] Three divergent poster-card components fragment the browse experience
- **File:** src/screens/HistoryScreen.web.jsx:145
- **Why:** Movies shelves/grid use PosterCard.web (skeleton sweep, rating badge, resume bar, injected hover-glow), Series uses TVPosterCard, and Home hand-rolls MyListCard + CWCard with aspectRatio (HistoryScreen.web:145,203) — the very property PosterCard.web:12 documents as broken on old webOS and replaced with explicit height. The same conceptual object looks and behaves subtly differently on each screen (different hover, skeleton, rating, and box-sizing), which is exactly the 'inconsistent component vocabulary across screens' the product bans.
- **Fix:** Converge Movies/Series/Home onto one PosterCard with props for the resume-bar/rating variants; retire MyListCard/CWCard/TVPosterCard in favor of the shared card so hover, focus, skeleton, and box model are identical everywhere.
- **Command:** $impeccable distill

### [P2] Muted secondary text fails AA contrast on elevated surfaces
- **File:** src/screens/SettingsScreen.web.jsx:110
- **Why:** colors.muted (#7A86A8) computes to ~4.05:1 on surface2 (#1B2236) — below the 4.5:1 body bar — and it is used for small (<18px) secondary text on surface2 cards: Settings unselected chip labels and ChipRow label (SettingsScreen.web:110,128), episode duration (SeriesDetail.web:286), and card meta. The team already caught this for the 10px LIVE dot (LiveTVScreen.web:149, overridden to textDim) but the pattern recurs. On bg it's ~4.9:1 (passes); on surface2 it fails.
- **Fix:** Use textDim (#B8C0DA) instead of muted for body-size secondary text that sits on surface/surface2, reserving muted for text on bg or for ≥18px.
- **Command:** $impeccable colorize

### [P2] Three different no-account empty states with divergent copy and CTAs
- **File:** src/screens/LiveTVScreen.web.jsx:607
- **Why:** LiveTV shows inline 'No account' / 'Tap "Accounts"…' / 'Add Account' (LiveTVScreen.web:611), Movies/Series show 'No account' / 'Add your media service from Settings' / 'Add Account', and History uses LABELS.noAccountTitle 'No account connected' / 'Connect account'. Same condition, three different titles, bodies, and button labels — the drift labels.js was created to prevent.
- **Fix:** Route all three through the labels.js noAccount* strings and a single StatePanel config.
- **Command:** $impeccable clarify

### [P2] Full-screen and grid loads use spinners where rails set a skeleton expectation
- **File:** src/screens/MoviesScreen.web.jsx:71
- **Why:** Shelf rails render SkeletonPoster placeholders, but the Movies/Series top-level load (StatePanel mode=loading), the CategoryPage grid initial load (MoviesScreen.web:72, SeriesScreen.web:321), and LiveTV shelves (LiveTVScreen.web:302) all show a centered Spinner. The design language calls for skeletons over spinners, and mixing the two within the same product makes the heavier loads feel less finished than the rails.
- **Fix:** Show a skeleton grid (reuse SkeletonPoster in the grid geometry) for category/full-screen loads so the loading vocabulary is consistent.
- **Command:** $impeccable animate

### [P2] Three modal mechanisms coexist
- **File:** src/screens/LiveTVScreen.web.jsx:686
- **Why:** Accounts and Settings use native <dialog>.showModal() (AppNavigator.web:751,804), Add-Channel uses a react-native Modal slide sheet (LiveTVScreen.web:686), and the exit prompt is a hand-rolled fixed div (AppNavigator.web:855). Three focus-trap/backdrop behaviors and three visual treatments for the same 'overlay' concept increases maintenance surface and subtly differs in dismissal semantics.
- **Fix:** Standardize on the native <dialog> pattern (which already gives focus trap + inert backdrop + focus restore) for all three, or wrap one shared Modal primitive.
- **Command:** $impeccable distill

### [P3] Discover pills carry a redundant trailing chevron
- **File:** src/presentation/components/DiscoverPills.web.jsx:76
- **Why:** Each DiscoverPills item shows a leading category icon, the label, AND a trailing chevron-right (DiscoverPills.web:74-76). The chevron implies a list-row drill-in affordance on what is a pill/chip; combined with the leading glyph it's visual redundancy that adds a little noise to an otherwise clean row.
- **Fix:** Drop the trailing chevron (the leading icon + pill shape already signals selectability) or keep only one directional cue.
- **Command:** $impeccable distill

## Persona Red Flags
- First-time playlist setup: hits the worst three moments in sequence — can't reach Accounts by keyboard, is told to 'add from Settings' where nothing exists, and if credentials are wrong gets a white OS alert() over the dark UI. The single most fragile journey in the app.
- Remote/keyboard-only viewer on desktop: stranded at the top nav (no tabbable tabs, Accounts, Settings, or Profile), and even where controls are reachable the focus ring is invisible during keyboard nav — the app feels broken before any content loads.
- Live-TV viewer on a flaky network: buffering/reconnect feedback is good, but there is no pointer volume or mute control for live streams and arrow keys change the channel instead of volume — muting a loud channel by mouse is impossible.

## Minor Observations
- prefers-reduced-motion zeroes animation duration app-wide (AppNavigator.web:51), which freezes the buffering spinner mid-rotation — motion-sensitive users lose the 'still working' cue; the 'Loading…/Reconnecting…' text saves it but consider a non-animated determinate/pulse indicator.
- The always-on keyboard-shortcut legend footer in the player (VideoPlayerScreen.web:1107) duplicates Settings > Keyboard shortcuts and adds standing density that edges toward the power-user anti-reference; consider showing it only briefly on control reveal.
- VideoPlayer 'Next episode' button rests at accentAlpha(0.9) indigo fill (nextBtn, line 84) — a near-full-saturation accent on a resting control; fine as an action but slightly loud against the Single-Light restraint elsewhere.
- PasswordInput eye toggle is ~34px wide (PasswordInput.jsx:41); hitSlop 8 pushes the effective target to ~50px so it clears 44 on mobile, but the visible target is under the touch floor.
- AuthScreen offers no password-recovery or help affordance; acceptable for a reseller-provisioned product, but a locked-out user has no in-app next step.
- Default profile avatar in the nav is the emoji 👤 (AppNavigator.web:413); avatars are intentionally identity-data glyphs elsewhere, but the fallback in chrome is the one spot an emoji stands in for a UI element.
