# Anti-Patterns Verdict

Not slop. A genuinely authored system with load-bearing comments, honored Single-Light discipline, and brand voice rendered at the high-stakes moments — a Netflix/Plex-fluent user would trust it. Verdict: authored.

**Detector:** The CLI detector exited 2 with 10 findings (1 overused-font Inter warning + 9 design-system-color advisories), all confirmed by source read; after applying the documented exemptions — Inter is the project's body font and all 9 colors are legitimate translucent rgba scrims/overlays/hover states — 0 represent genuine design drift. Notably the detector's color pass missed the one real raw-hex offender (#555 placeholder at SeriesScreen.web.jsx:279) and the semantic indigo-at-rest drift, both of which the design review caught.

# Cognitive Load

Low-to-moderate. Passes 6-7 of 8 checks: single focus, grouping, hierarchy, working-memory preservation (Continue Watching), and progressive disclosure are handled well. Two strains: the video-player settings popover rows exceed the 4-item working-memory limit (Speed ~6 chips, Aspect 5, Quality variable; VideoPlayerScreen.web.jsx:779,825), and the top nav presents 4 tabs + 3 icon actions = 7 simultaneous targets (AppNavigator.web.jsx:305-397) — grouped, so borderline not critical.

# What's Working

- Playback-failure reassurance is best-in-class: FATAL_HEADLINE leads with a calm benefit-first sentence and demotes the raw engine reason to quiet steel secondary text, with Retry + Close (VideoPlayerScreen.web.jsx:300, 697-724).
- Deferred, undoable removal protects cross-device state: a x doesn't hit the store for ~5s, an aria-live snackbar offers Undo, and an unmount still honors the intent (HistoryScreen.web.jsx:336-380, 638-701).
- Accessibility is wired in, not bolted on: cards/pills/toggles are role=button/switch with tabIndex and Enter/Space handlers, aria-labels throughout, a .keyboard-nav body class suppresses hover for Tab users, and StatePanel uses role=status/alert live regions (PosterCard.web.jsx:81-90, SettingsScreen.web.jsx:49-60, AppNavigator.web.jsx:470-479).
- prefers-reduced-motion is respected globally (AppNavigator.web.jsx:51); motion stays 120-320ms and state-only.
- Detector-clean color/font hygiene: all 10 CLI findings resolve to legitimate translucent scrims/overlays and the documented Inter body font — no genuine token drift in the scanned surface.

# Priority Issues

**[P1] Four divergent horizontal-rail implementations: Movies uses ContentShelf.web + PosterCard.web; Series uses bespoke ShelfBase + TVPosterCard; LiveTV uses LiveShelf + LiveCard; History hand-rolls MyListCard/CWCard with inline rails. Drag-scroll logic is copy-pasted 4x; focus-glow, skeleton sweep, and rating badges exist only on some.**
- Why: Consistency is the product's stated virtue. The Series shelf-title chevron is indigo (SeriesScreen.web.jsx:149) while the identical Movies control is muted (ContentShelf.web.jsx:118) — the same object behaves differently, the core heuristic-4 failure.
- Fix: Collapse onto one ContentShelf that takes a renderItem (already supported at ContentShelf.web.jsx:151) and delete the bespoke rails.
- Command: $impeccable distill — SeriesScreen.web.jsx:26-222, LiveTVScreen.web.jsx:169-347, HistoryScreen.web.jsx:497-636, ContentShelf.web.jsx:151

**[P2] Single-Light drift: indigo (colors.accent #6C5CE7) at rest in chrome — Back-pill glyph + label, Series shelf chevron, LiveCard channel abbreviation, season headers and episode-number badges.**
- Why: DESIGN.md's Single-Light Rule reserves indigo for the active path; these decorative resting tints spend the saturation budget the theater metaphor depends on. (Note: detector B's 9 color findings were all exempt translucent scrims and did NOT catch this — it's a semantic drift, not a raw-value one.)
- Fix: Move resting glyphs/labels to steel (muted) or ice (text); keep indigo for the genuinely-active item only.
- Command: $impeccable colorize — MovieDetail.web.jsx:42-47, SeriesDetail.web.jsx:40-47,240,264, SeriesScreen.web.jsx:149, LiveTVScreen.web.jsx:103

**[P2] SeriesDetail meta chips fail AA over backdrop, plus a hardcoded placeholder hex. MovieDetail deliberately fills its year/genre/age chips with opaque colors.surface for guaranteed AA (MovieDetail.web.jsx:196); SeriesDetail's equivalent chips are border-only, no fill (SeriesDetail.web.jsx:392-425), so muted steel over arbitrary photographic backdrop fails WCAG AA. Separately, SeriesScreen's CategoryPage search uses placeholderTextColor #555 (SeriesScreen.web.jsx:279), a raw hex bypassing colors.faint.**
- Why: WCAG 2.1 AA is a hard bar per PRODUCT.md, and #555 breaks the token discipline. Detector B flagged only translucent overlays (all false positives) and MISSED this literal #555 — a case where the design review caught what the detector did not.
- Fix: Mirror MovieDetail's scrim fill on Series chips; replace #555 with colors.faint.
- Command: $impeccable harden — SeriesDetail.web.jsx:392-425, MovieDetail.web.jsx:196, SeriesScreen.web.jsx:279

# Persona Red Flags

- **Casey (touch-web):** Shelf prev/next arrows are opacity:0 until :hover (AppNavigator.web.jsx:104-109) — on touch there's no hover, so they never appear. Drag-scroll uses mousedown/mousemove/pageX only (ContentShelf.web.jsx:66-84, HistoryScreen.web.jsx:30-61), not touch/pointer events, leaving touch users with native inertial scroll and no visible horizontal-nav affordance. Layout is desktop-first (MAX_W 1700, ss() at 1920 reference, 320px CW cards, 48px gutters) — oversized on phone widths. Hit targets themselves are fine (44px).
- **Sam (a11y):** Two real gaps under otherwise strong coverage. (1) The player seek bar is a div with onClick and no role=slider, aria-valuenow, or element-level key handling (VideoPlayerScreen.web.jsx:927) — AT users can't scrub via the control itself (volume is a real input[type=range], good). (2) Rails mount only a window (ContentShelf slice start..end; VirtualGrid range) so Tab order shifts and focus can be dropped as cards unmount on scroll.
- **Morgan (cross-device continuity):** The 'Synced across your devices' hero label (HistoryScreen.web.jsx:489) and resume bars are strong continuity peaks, but the silent LiveTV category loss directly undercuts the same-library-every-screen promise, and continuity is only stated never shown — no 'watched on your phone 2h ago', no device/last-seen context on the CW card.
