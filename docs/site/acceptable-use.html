Acceptable Use Policy

Suvo · Last updated 8 July 2026

Suvo is a media player that plays a playlist you supply. It provides no content of its own. Because the content comes from you, this policy sets out what you may and may not do with the app. It forms part of the Terms of Service.

The rule of thumb: only load content you have the legal right to access and play. Suvo is a tool; how you use it is your responsibility.

Your content is yours to account for

You are solely responsible for the playlists, streams, and media you load into Suvo. By using the app you confirm that you have the necessary rights, licenses, or permissions for that content in your jurisdiction.

You may not use Suvo to

  • Access, store, stream, or distribute content you don’t have the legal right to use.
  • Infringe the copyright, trademark, or other intellectual-property rights of anyone.
  • Circumvent access controls, geographic restrictions, or digital-rights-management protections.
  • Break any applicable law or regulation, or the terms of your own playlist or media provider.
  • Use the app to harm, harass, or violate the rights of others.

We supply no content — and can’t police yours

Suvo neither hosts nor provides any channels, streams, or media, and has no visibility into or control over the content you load or the providers you connect to. Nothing in the app should be taken as an endorsement of any content or provider.

Copyright & infringement reports

We respect intellectual-property rights. Because we don’t host content, most concerns are best raised with the provider that hosts the material. If you believe the app itself is being promoted or used to enable infringement, contact us at support@suvo.tv with:

  • A description of the work or right you believe is being infringed.
  • The specifics of what you’re reporting and where it can be found.
  • Your contact details and a statement that your report is made in good faith.

Enforcement

If you use Suvo in a way that breaks this policy or the law, we may suspend or terminate your account and, where appropriate, cooperate with lawful requests. See the Terms of Service for the full picture.
