Data Usage

Suvo · Last updated 8 July 2026

This page is the plain-language companion to our Privacy Policy — a straight answer to “what data does Suvo actually collect, and what happens to it?” It also mirrors what we declare in the Apple App Privacy and Google Play Data Safety forms.

None of your data is used for advertising, tracking, or profiling, and none of it is sold or shared with data brokers. Suvo collects only what it needs to play your playlist and, if you opt in, sync your own data across your devices.

What Suvo does not collect

  • No advertising identifiers, and no advertising SDKs.
  • No location data.
  • No contacts, photos, or files beyond the playlist you enter.
  • No cross-app or cross-site tracking.
  • No sale or brokering of any data.

Where your data lives

  • No account: everything stays on your device.
  • Signed in: the data above is stored in our sync backend (Supabase) solely so it’s available on your other devices.

Streaming traffic goes to your provider

When you press play, the app connects directly to the playlist provider you configured. Your streaming traffic and credentials go to that provider under their own terms and privacy policy — not through us. See Acceptable Use for what that means for the content you load.

Retention & deletion

Local data is removed when you clear it in the app or uninstall. Account data is deleted on request or when you delete your account. To request deletion, email support@suvo.tv.

Questions

Contact support@suvo.tv. For the full policy, see the Privacy Policy.
